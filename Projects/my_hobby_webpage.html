1. Basil

Ok, so, first up is basil. A flowery, fragrant herb that makes almost any dish taste amazing.
It's used in everything from pasta to pizza (basically any dish with an Italian taste profile)
and it's also super easy to grow. Below is a plant 'pokedex' to help break things down:

Basil Basics

Basil loves: 👍

  • Warm sunny weather!
    If temperatures frost bring your plant inside.

  • Tomatoes!
    Growing next to tomatoes may help your basil grow better.

  • Water!
    Basil loves water but be careful to only water the base of the plant as perpetually wet leaves are prone to disease.

Basil hates: 👎

  • The cold!
    If temperatures tend to frost where you live, bring your plant inside.

  • Being too crowded!
    Give your plant adequate space as not enough air can cause mildew to grow on your basil.

  • Ants
    These bastards will eat your basil and leave the leaves looking like swiss cheese.

2. Mint

Ah, so you chose mint, the shade lover. A crisp, fresh, fragrant herb that adds a cool
note of elegance to any cocktail (gimlets, anyone?) and pairs well with deserts too!
Plant your mint in loose, loamy potting soil and water adequately. Place in a cool, shady, spot out of direct sunlight.

Mint Basics

Mint loves: 👍

  • The cold!
    If temperatures tend to frost where you live, bring your plant inside.

  • Water!
    Give your plant enough water as one hot day without adequate irrigation could cause your mint to die.

Mint hates: 👎

  • Hot weather!
    If temperatures tend to be really hot and dry where you live, consider moving your mint to the shade.

  • Being too crowded!
    Mint tends to be invasive so keep it in it's own container. It needs room to g r o w and will take over an area if you let it!

  • Ants! ...Again!
    Ants love eating basil and mint. Watch for little holes in the leaves for signs of an ant invasion.


Materials You May Need: 🔨

  • Garden soil (if planting in ground)



Top Tips ⭐

As mentioned before there are challenges and threats for every gardener!
From ants to mildew, -you- are the guardian of your garden. Below are some helpful tips to help overcome them!

Ants

Ants are annoying.
They come, help themselves to your herbs, they leave. It sucks. Watch for holes in your leaves like the ones in the picture below.
If your plants are being taken over by ants, consider adding beneficial insects to your garden like a praying mantis or ladybugs.




Garden Layout

Be a pro gardener by being mindful of your garden's layout.
Place plants that are sensitive to heat and sun in the shade, and plants that require full sun out in a sunny area facing south.




Fertilizer + Mulching

A master gardener's secret? Fertilizing and mulching.
While it may not seem that important, it is. Adding fertilizer helps keep your plants healthy and vibrant. Adding mulch helps retain moisture and saves water!
